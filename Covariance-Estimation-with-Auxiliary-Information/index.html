    Covariance Estimation with Auxiliary Information
    • Model set-up
    • Auxiliary Information

    Model set-up

    We have generated a $800*800$ block diagnoal population covaraince $\Sigma$, with each block, $\sigma_{ij} = \rho^{\lvert{i - j}\rvert}$. Here $\rho = 0.99$. The following figure shows the covariance matrix.

    We randomly generate $T = 100$ observations of $X \sim N(0,\Sigma)$. And use this to construct the following estimator:

    1. Sample covariance: $S = \frac{1}{T}XX'$;
    2. Ledoit-Wolf linear shrinkage estimator: $S_{lw}$.

    Auxiliary Information

    Assume in addition to $X$, we also observe a network $G$ that indicates the existence of link between $i,j$. Here we let $$G_{ij} = 1 \quad \text{if} \quad i \neq j, \quad \sigma_{ij} > 0.7 $$

    Then we can construct two new estimators:

    1. Hard thresholding estimator with $G$ : $S_G$ where we only keep those elements $S_{ij}$ in the sample covariance $S$ such that $G_{ij} = 1$.
    2. $S_{new}$, which is shrinkage with two targets: we construct a linear combination of $I$, $S$, $S_G$ such that the MSE of estimating $\Sigma$ is minimized. Details of Calculation.

    The errors of estimation for matrix 1-norm is:

    0 1
    0 Norm of Population Covariance 344.676
    1 Estimation error of S 123.392
    2 Estimation error of S_lw 100.27
    3 Estimation error of S_G 254.238
    4 Estimation error of S_new 96.1036

    The erro for Frobinius Norm is:

    0 1
    0 Norm of Population Covariance 373.378
    1 Estimation error of S 88.3676
    2 Estimation error of S_lw 79.8294
    3 Estimation error of S_G 252.26
    4 Estimation error of S_new 74.9107

    We have tried different settings with different values of $\rho$, $N$, $T$, the results are similar.
